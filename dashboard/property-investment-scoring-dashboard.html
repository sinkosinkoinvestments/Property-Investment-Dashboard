Glossary & Definitions

Key metrics and scoring criteria used in the dashboard algorithm

2nd Dwelling Room A heuristic assessing if the property can fit a second dwelling or granny flat. Rated "High Potential" if land is >1000m² and usable. "Needs Review" if the land is large but flagged for poor usability (e.g. slope or terrain).
Cap Rate (Capitalisation Rate) The expected rate of return generated on a real estate investment property based on current income. Calculated as Net Operating Income (NOI) divided by the property's purchase price. Higher is generally better for pure cash flow.
DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) A measure of cash flow available to pay current debt obligations. A DSCR > 1.0 means the property generates enough income to cover its debt payments entirely on its own.
10-Yr IRR (Internal Rate of Return) The annualized effective compounded return rate over a 10-year holding period. It accounts for rent growth, capital growth over time, debt paydown, and the eventual sale price of the asset.
Cash-on-Cash Return The annual pre-tax cash flow divided by the total actual cash invested (deposit, stamp duty, renovation costs). It measures the immediate cash income earned on the exact cash you put into the deal.
Net Weekly Cashflow The out-of-pocket surplus or deficit per week after all expenses, interest, property management, and taxes are accounted for. A positive number means the property pays you to own it.
Value-Add Bonuses "Usable Land", "Subdivision Potential", and "Dual Living / Granny Flat" are binary flags that indicate the property has physical characteristics allowing for manufacturing equity or adding a secondary income stream, triggering bonus points in the score.
